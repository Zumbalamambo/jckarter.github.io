I has a web blog

updated December 30, 2008 20:42:46 PDT

The winter storms compromised my immune system, and I came down with a bad case of NIH syndrome. While I was in bed recovering I wrote my own blog engine. Check out these awesome features!

  • Text editor integration with any text editor you want: Emacs, Textmate, Vi… even Notepad. Hell, even Word. Anything that can edit text files.
  • Secure. No logins to crack. No javascript to exploit. No forms to feed spam and malformed HTML into.
  • High performance, limited only by your operating system's I/O abilities.
  • Easy, universal deployment—just rsync a directory full of static HTML pages to your webserver. Your hosting provider can do static HTML, right?

"Static HTML?!" you gasp, the long-suppressed odor of <marquee> tags briefly tickling your nose. "But what about my comments? What about my 'Last 50 Twitters' box, the trucker hat and Members Only jacket of any trendy blog? Oh dear, where shall I put all of my 'Submit to ___' buttons?" Humbug! If an article's worth discussing, reddit will host comments for you. And tinselling your text with favicons for news sites, like a rebellious youth diaper-pinning band patches to their camo jacket, well, I just don't get that. Anyone who uses digg or whatever already knows how to submit articles, right? And you're only hurting yourself with all those Latest Twits, Facebook Status, Currently Listening To, and Sexy Fanfic of the Day boxes. Your content's already competing for attention against every other self-important solipsist on the Internet. Why do you want it to compete against noise on your own site?
