Fuck it, we'll do it a priori

updated December 28, 2008 23:33:33 PDT

Your typical game physics engine, be it old school sprite overlap detection or a state-of-the-art 3D physics powerhouse like Havok, does what folks in the biz† call a posteriori collision detection. Every object in the game gets a physics credit card, allowing them to charge forward in their current direction for a full timer tick but sticking them with a big fat transfer of momentum bill at the end of the game loop iteration. Creative accountants find the objects that manage to overlap each other and fudge the numbers so it looks like they were following the laws of physics. Depending on the engine, an object might beat the system by traveling so fast it gets from one side of an obstacle to the other before the bill arrives in the mail.

† And by "folks in the biz" I mean "Wikipedia".
